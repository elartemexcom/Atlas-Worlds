A spatial engine for visual meaning, not a folder system

Place tags. Turn meaning into gravity. Let images settle.

Atlas is a visual organization system where tags live in an XY field, and your images arrange themselves underneath based on how closely they match those tags and each other. When forces overlap, stalagmites form: ranked columns of images that belong to that blend.

The point is simple: stop asking “where is the file?” and start asking “what belongs near this idea?”

What you do

Drag tags around the field, adjust heat, optionally add one or more anchor images. Watch formations appear beneath intersections.

What Atlas does

Converts each image into a similarity fingerprint, then resolves placement in a spatial field so your archive becomes a navigable landscape.

Why it feels different

Lists are linear. Atlas is relational. It shows gradients, clusters, and crossroads you would never discover in folders.

How it works

Atlas has one central trick: an XY tag field that creates vertical formations of images underneath. You sculpt the field. The archive responds.

  1. Place tags on the XY field (Texture, Minimal, Grain, Soft Light, Vintage, etc.).
  2. Move tags closer to strengthen their blend. Move them apart to loosen the blend.
  3. Heat controls how much a tag “absorbs” images into its influence.
  4. Images settle underneath the field at positions that best match the active tag geometry.
  5. Where influences overlap strongly, a stalagmite column forms: top = best matches, bottom = weaker matches.
  6. Optionally add one or multiple anchor images to bias formations toward a specific seed aesthetic.

Vocabulary that makes the system click

These terms are not fluff. They are handles for the mental model so anyone can understand what’s happening.

XY Tag Field A 2D plane where tags are placed like bodies in a map. Distance matters. Close tags blend strongly. Far tags blend softly.
Heat The strength of a tag’s pull. Default heat is neutral, meaning the field shows the most balanced placement. Increase heat to prioritize that tag and absorb more images into its influence.
Stalagmite Column A vertical ranked stack of images that forms beneath a strong intersection of influences. Top = closest matches. Down the column = increasing divergence.
Anchor Image One or more images you select as seeds. They become additional attractors that bias the field and reshape formations.
Column Pairing A comparison mode: select two columns (often neighbors) and lock them side-by-side to see how tiny field shifts change the ranked results.
Layer Overview A way to view “slices” of the system at different depths or perspectives. Think: you can inspect the top of the landscape or descend layer-by-layer.
Column Signature A compact identity marker for a column that records its coordinate and how it relates to all active tags and anchors. This makes formations reproducible and explainable.
Relational Descriptor A readable breakdown showing how strongly an image aligns with each tag. It answers “why is this here?” without guesswork.

A simple example

Place Texture close to Dark, keep Minimal farther away, then raise heat on Texture. Atlas will form a tighter column that favors gritty, darker images, even pulling in moderate matches that previously sat elsewhere.

What “default heat” means

With equal heat across tags, Atlas shows the most neutral field equilibrium. When you change heat, you are intentionally biasing which concepts should dominate formation.

Z-Dimension Layer Overview

View the landscape from the top, then descend. At the top, you see the purest matches. As you move downward, you reveal more variation and divergence.

  • Global dial: reveals the cluster depth layer-by-layer or all at once.
  • Local dial: click a column and traverse image-by-image by rank, including numeric jump.
  • Each image can display its coordinate and relational descriptor to show why it belongs.

X-Dimension Layer Overview

A side-on perspective that shows how formations distribute laterally across the field. This helps you see neighboring columns, compare them, and understand how the field geometry shapes outcomes.

  • Ideal for column pairing and inspecting transitions between nearby intersections.
  • Shows the “shape” of meaning, not just the top-ranked results.
  • Reveals where overlaps create stronger or weaker formations.

The scope and beauty of the project

Atlas is designed as a foundation that scales from a practical organizer to a full spatial intelligence system. Here is the clean progression.

Atlas 1.0: Plain Atlas XY Tag Field + Heat + Stalagmite Columns + optional single or multi-anchor images. The core gravity engine.
Atlas 2.0: Atlas Sphere The same engine constrained to a globe. Meaning becomes geography: coordinates, regions, local neighborhoods, and navigable worlds.
Atlas 3.0: Atlas Dimensions Dimensional overviews, deeper explainability, and comparative analysis. Z-layer traversal, X-side inspection, and column-by-column pairing with signatures.

The promise

Atlas makes your archive behave like a living universe: you do not organize once and hope it stays useful. You sculpt meaning on demand, and the library rearranges itself to match the moment.

Tag placement (XY) + Tag strength (Heat) + Optional anchors (Images) ↓ Resolved intersections ↓ Stalagmite columns (ranked) ↓ Layer navigation + column comparison + explainable signatures
