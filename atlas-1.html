The model

What moving tags actually does

Tags aren’t labels. They are forces. Their positions create a geometry of meaning.

Close tags create tighter intersections: fewer images qualify, but they match more precisely.
Far tags create softer intersections: more images qualify, with broader variety.

With equal Heat, the field is neutral. When you increase a tag’s heat, you’re telling Atlas: “Care more about this concept.” That expands its basin and absorbs more images into its orbit.

Glossary

Unique terms that make Atlas instantly graspable

These names are “handles” for the mental model. They’re designed for quick comprehension.

XY Tag Field
the map

Tags live on a plane. Their geometry defines what combinations become “strong” intersections.

Heat
the power knob

Heat is tag strength. Default heat is neutral equilibrium. Higher heat expands that tag’s basin and pulls in more images.

Stalagmite
the formation

A vertical ranked column of images under an intersection. Top = strongest matches. Deeper = more divergence.

Anchor Image(s)
the seed(s)

One or multiple images used as additional attractors that bias the field. This creates branched dimensions of meaning.

Relational Descriptor
the why

A readable breakdown of how strongly an image aligns with every active tag in the field.

Column Signature
the fingerprint

A compact marker encoding: tag positions, tag heats, anchor context, and the column’s identity so it can be compared and revisited.
