Atlas v2.0 — Spherical Meaning Space

The archive becomes a Sphere, and you become a Cartographer of Meaning.

Atlas Sphere simplifies the entire Atlas idea by switching from a flat tag-plane to a bounded, navigable world. You place tags and anchor images on the surface. The system then mathematically places every image inside the sphere where it best approximates those surface forces.

Why a sphere?

Because it prevents “infinite sprawl.” The Sphere is a world with edges, routes, and regions. It keeps meaning discoverable, revisitable, and easy to navigate without getting lost.

What it is

Atlas Sphere, explained in plain language

Imagine your entire image collection living inside a glass globe. Around the surface are “meaning markers” (tags) and “aesthetic anchors” (images you choose). The globe pulls every image into the spot where it makes the most sense.

Boundary

A finite world (no sprawl)

Instead of endlessly expanding space, the Sphere forces all meaning into a navigable volume. Regions stay findable.

Surface logic

Tags + anchors live on the surface

You place concepts and seed aesthetics on the outside, so the inside can “settle” in response.

Natural clusters

Clusters appear as geography

The sphere fills with visual continents, dense cities, corridors, and quiet deserts of rarity.

How it works

Best approximation placement, inside a sphere

Atlas Sphere places each image where it best fits the combined pull of the surface. In practice, that means each image is scored against:

The output is a coordinate inside the sphere where the image “belongs.” Similar images land near each other automatically, producing clusters you can navigate like geography.

Why it simplifies

One shape, one rule

The Sphere reduces complexity by making everything a placement problem: put meaning on the surface, let the archive settle inside.

No fragile folder trees, no “where did I put that.” If it matches your surface forces, it appears where you’d intuitively look.

Controls

Heat, interaction, and direct exploration

Atlas Sphere stays neutral by default: equal heat produces the most faithful map of your archive’s natural structure. Then you can bend the world intentionally.

Heat

Heat pulls mass

Increase a tag’s heat to pull more images toward that concept, even if it means stealing them from neighboring concepts. You are literally reshaping the geography.

Click

Click inside the sphere

Click any visible cluster point to open a neighborhood view: the closest set of images around that coordinate. It’s “local browsing” without search terms.

Anchors

Anchor images steer style

Add anchor images to bias the entire interior. The sphere becomes an aesthetic world orbiting your chosen seeds.

Navigation

Never get lost: bookmarks + annotations

Atlas Sphere treats clusters as places you can name, save, and return to. Users can bookmark regions, annotate clusters, and build a personal map of the globe.

A cluster bookmark can store:
Sphere Coordinate Surface Layout Heat Settings Cluster Members Notes

This is why the tagline matters: we are cartographers of meaning. The user isn’t just viewing images, they’re charting a world.
